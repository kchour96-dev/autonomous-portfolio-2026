Root Cause Analysis

The NetNut/Popa botnet operated by secretly enrolling millions of consumer devices (smart TVs, streaming boxes) into a residential proxy network, often without user consent. This infrastructure was then resold and white-labeled, heavily utilized by cybercriminals to mask their malicious traffic, thereby bypassing traditional security measures and making attribution difficult. The root cause lies in insecure IoT device ecosystems and deceptive practices in app distribution.
